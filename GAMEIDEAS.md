## Brief About Me
I have buried hundreds of ideas for games and apps in dozens of notebooks since I was a kid in the 2000s. I suppressed my desire to make games for a variety of no-good reasons, listening to other people telling me that it wouldn't be a profitable career, that I'd be overworked, and other very stupid opinions. I attended a two-year long academy in 2011-2013 for software & game development and made a handful of games and prototypes but erroneously chose to pursue general Computer Science education and eventually website development over the decade instead of what I really wanted to pursue.

I hid my ideas in notebooks afraid other people would take them but I eventually realized (1) it is very unlikely anyone would take my ideas or create them effectively, (2) if they did then I get to enjoy my game ideas as a player, (3) teams could've recognized my ideas and reached out to me for opportunities, and (4) it's hard to tell people my suppressed passion has been game design when I have nothing online to show for it. Who knows where I'd be if I did - and there's always a possibility my ideas aren't even that great and they wouldn't have taken me anywhere! I've just spent so much of my creative inspiration on imagining huge & complex worlds, imaginary civilizations with cultures and languages, interesting & unique gameplay mechanics (that were later coincidentally implemented in games years later), etc.

I managed to make a few prototypes & little games but spent my life thus-far either playing games, pursuing a soulless career consulting in enterprise website development, or experiencing much of what else life has had to offer (which I don't really regret).
